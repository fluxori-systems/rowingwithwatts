### MET Formula

  

Total Calories burned in 1 minute = (3.5 x MET x body weight in kg) / 200

  

The MET (Metabolic Equivalent of Task) formula helps estimate how many calories you burn during different activities.

 

One MET is the energy you use at rest. Activities are measured as multiples of this resting rate. For example, light indoor rowing at a MET value of 5.0 burns five times the energy you use sitting still.

 

To calculate calories burned, the formula needs three values: the MET value of the activity, your weight in kilograms, and the duration in minutes. The constant 3.5 is resting oxygen uptake in ml/kg/min, and 200 is a unit conversion that accounts for the caloric value of oxygen. Accuracy is typically within ±10–20% for most adults (Byrne et al., 2005).

 

Here are the 2024 MET values for indoor rowing from the Adult Compendium of Physical Activities:

 
 
- **5.0 MET** — Rowing, stationary ergometer, less than 100 watts (Light)
- **7.5 MET** — Rowing, stationary, 100 to 149 watts (Moderate)
- **11.0 MET** — Rowing, stationary, 150 to 199 watts (Vigorous)
- **14.0 MET** — Rowing, stationary, 200 watts or more (Hard)
- **15.5 MET** — Rowing, ergometer, racing speed, 32+ strokes per minute (Maximum)
 

### Concept2 Weight-Adjusted Formula

 

When you enter your 500m split time into the calculator, it switches to a more precise method developed from Concept2's official calorie calculation.

 

The standard Concept2 PM display shows calories without accounting for your body weight — it assumes a reference weight. The weight-adjusted version corrects for this:

 

**Step 1 — Convert split to watts:**

watts = 2.80 ÷ (split in seconds ÷ 500)³

 

This is derived from the physics of the Concept2 flywheel, where power output scales with the cube of velocity.

 

**Step 2 — Calculate weight-adjusted calories:**

- PM cal/hr = (4 × watts × 0.8604) + 300
- Adjusted cal/hr = PM cal/hr − 300 + (1.714 × body weight in lbs)
- Total kcal = Adjusted cal/hr × (duration in minutes ÷ 60)

 

This formula is sourced from Concept2's official documentation and adjusts for the fact that heavier rowers burn more calories at the same power output. If you know your split, this method gives a more personalised result than MET alone.

### Our Rowing Intensity Selections

To make calorie calculations more user-friendly, we have categorized rowing intensity into five levels based on maximum heart rate (MHR) and strokes per minute (SPM). 

 

Each intensity level corresponds to a range of MET values from the Compendium.

 

#### **1. Light: Active Recovery**

 
 
- **Intensity:** 55-70% MHR 
- **SPM:** 18-20 
- **Corresponding MET:** 5.0 METs 
- **Description:** This level represents a low effort, typically for active recovery, engaging in moderate effort rowing on a stationary ergometer. 
 

#### **2. Moderate: Endurance**

 
 
- **Intensity:** 71-80% MHR 
- **SPM:** 20-24 
- **Corresponding MET:** 7.5 METs 
- **Description:** This level involves maintaining a steady pace, suitable for building endurance and rowing at a general vigorous effort. 
 

#### **3. Vigorous: Anaerobic Threshold**

 
 
- **Intensity:** 81-90% MHR 
- **SPM:** 24-28 
- **Corresponding MET:** 11.0 METs 
- **Description:** At this intensity, rowing becomes more challenging, focusing on crossing the anaerobic threshold with higher power output. 
 

#### **4. Hard: VO2 Max**

 
 
- **Intensity:** 90-94% MHR 
- **SPM:** 28-32 
- **Wattage:** ≥ 200W
- **Corresponding MET:** 14.0 METs 
- **Description:** This high-intensity level targets maximal oxygen uptake. It requires 200 watts or more on the ergometer — demanding work that most rowers can sustain only in shorter intervals. 
 

#### **5. Maximum: Anaerobic**

 
 
- **Intensity:** 95-100% MHR 
- **SPM:** 32+ 
- **Wattage:** Racing pace
- **Corresponding MET:** 15.5 METs 
- **Description:** The most intense level, where rowing is performed at racing speeds — 32 or more strokes per minute — and energy comes primarily from anaerobic pathways. This zone is defined by stroke rate and all-out effort rather than a fixed wattage. 
 

### Integrating MET Values with Rowing Intensity

 

By aligning 2024 Compendium MET values with our five rowing intensity levels, we give you a research-backed calorie estimate based on how hard you're actually working.

 

Select your intensity, enter your weight and duration, and the calculator does the rest. Both methods give you a solid estimate for tracking your training and setting calorie goals. No single formula is exact for every individual, but these are the most accurate publicly available methods for indoor rowing.
