## Calculator Calculations

 

We used the MET formula and [2024 data](https://pacompendium.com/) from the Adult Compendium of Physical Activities to create the calorie calculations for indoor rowing.

 

### MET Formula

  

Total Calories burned in 1 minute = (3.5 x MET x body weight in kg)/200

  

The MET (Metabolic Equivalent of Task) formula helps estimate how many calories you burn during different activities. 

 

One MET is the energy you use at rest. Activities are measured as multiples of this resting rate. For example, an activity like light indoor rowing with a MET value of 5 uses five times the energy you do at rest.

 

To calculate calories burned using the MET formula, you need three values: the MET value of the activity, your weight in kilograms, and the duration of the activity in hours. 

 

Here are the 2024 MET values for Indoor rowing:

 
 
- 5.0 (MET Value): Rowing, stationary ergometer, general, less than 100 watts, moderate effort  
- 7.5 (MET Value): Rowing, stationary, 100 to 149 watts, vigorous effort  
- 11.0 (MET Value): Rowing, stationary, 150 to 199 watts, vigorous effort  
- 14.0 (MET Value): Rowing, stationary, 200+ watts, very vigorous effort  
- 15.5 (MET Value): Rowing, single scull, ergometer, ergometer on slides, racing speed (32+ strokes/min, more than 10 mph) 
 

### Rowing Intensity

 

The rowing intensity selections in the start and results we created pulling together multiple sources and our using our expertise.

 
 
1. Light: Active Recovery | 55-70 % MHR | 18-20 SPM 
2. Moderate: Endurance | 71-80 % MHR | 20-24 SPM 
3. Vigorous: Anaerobic Threshold | 81-90 % MHR | 24-28 SPM 
4. Hard: VO2 Max | 90-94 % MHR | 28-32 SPM 
5. Maximum: Anaerobic (without oxygen) | 95-100 % MHR | 32+ SPM 
 

MHR refers to maximum heart rate and SPM is strokes per minute.

 

These various bands represent a combination from traditional rowing training zones, while incorporating them into the new MET values.

 

### Our Rowing Intensity Selections

 

![Rowing Machine Calorie Calculator Graphical image on Rowing Intensity Selections breakdown with MET MHR and SPM](/uploads/2024/05/Rowing-Machine-Calorie-Calculator-Graphical-image-on-Rowing-Intensity-Selections-breakdown-with-MET-MHR-and-SPM-1024x638.webp)

 

To make calorie calculations more user-friendly, we have categorized rowing intensity into five levels based on maximum heart rate (MHR) and strokes per minute (SPM). 

 

Each intensity level corresponds to a range of MET values from the Compendium.

 

#### **1. Light: Active Recovery**

 
 
- **Intensity:** 55-70% MHR 
- **SPM:** 18-20 
- **Corresponding MET:** 5.0 METs 
- **Description:** This level represents a low effort, typically for active recovery, engaging in moderate effort rowing on a stationary ergometer. 
 

#### **2. Moderate: Endurance**

 
 
- **Intensity:** 71-80% MHR 
- **SPM:** 20-24 
- **Corresponding MET:** 7.5 METs 
- **Description:** This level involves maintaining a steady pace, suitable for building endurance and rowing at a general vigorous effort. 
 

#### **3. Vigorous: Anaerobic Threshold**

 
 
- **Intensity:** 81-90% MHR 
- **SPM:** 24-28 
- **Corresponding MET:** 11.0 METs 
- **Description:** At this intensity, rowing becomes more challenging, focusing on crossing the anaerobic threshold with higher power output. 
 

#### **4. Hard: VO2 Max**

 
 
- **Intensity:** 90-94% MHR 
- **SPM:** 28-32 
- **Corresponding MET:** 14.0 METs 
- **Description:** This high-intensity level targets maximal oxygen uptake, rowing at 150 to 199 watts or greater, requiring significant effort. 
 

#### **5. Maximum: Anaerobic**

 
 
- **Intensity:** 95-100% MHR 
- **SPM:** 32+ 
- **Corresponding MET:** 15.5 METs 
- **Description:** The most intense level, where rowing is done at racing speeds, exceeding 200 watts and focusing on anaerobic performance. 
 

### Integrating MET Values with Rowing Intensity

 

By aligning traditional MET values with our rowing intensity levels, we estimate calories burned more accurately.

 

Users select their intensity level, input their weight and rowing duration, and use the MET values to calculate calories burned.

 

This approach simplifies understanding and using MET values. It makes it easier for rowers to manage fitness goals and track progress.
