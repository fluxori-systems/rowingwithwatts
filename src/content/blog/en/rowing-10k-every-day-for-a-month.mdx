I rowed 10,000 m every day for 30 days and tracked all the data from the rowing machine, my daily health stats and my diet.

To get the most comprehensive view of the impact from rowing on my body, I did before and after body assessments that ended up giving some unexpected results.

These assessments included:

- Photos
- Biometric evaluation
- Data on vital biomarkers
- ECG analysis
- Data on my heart/autonomic nervous system.

**Key Takeaways**

- I lost **13 lbs / 5.9 kg**
- I burned **19,858 Calories**
- My 10k time improved by **9 minutes 22 seconds**
- I completed **300,000 meters** on the Rowing Machine

## Before starting the challenge

[Patellofemoral pain](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/patellofemoral-pain-syndrome/symptoms-causes/syc-20350792) — that's how the idea behind this challenge started. Also known as runner's knee.

I had run my first marathon and had a niggle in my knee that would not go away. The physiotherapist recommended taking a break and doing low-impact exercises for a while.

I had done a few sessions on the rowing machine before, 500 m sprints and a 2k row for time. So I decided to challenge myself and complete 10,000 m every day for 30 days and document the whole journey.

Definitely not what my physio had in mind.

Little did I know how much I would fall in love with indoor rowing.

### InBody

The [InBody](https://inbody.com/en) assessment provides a thorough analysis of your weight, body composition, height, and BMI.

When you stand on the machine and hold the handles, it runs a weak electrical current through your body. This allows it to measure the current's changes known as Bioelectrical Impedance Analysis.

<img src="/uploads/2024/01/Inbody-Machine-Model-270-768x1024.webp" alt="Inbody Machine - Model 270. A digital scale placed on a wooden floor." class="max-w-xs mx-auto block rounded-lg my-6" loading="lazy" />

Modern assessment machines provide good results but aren't in a completely controlled environment, so there may be discrepancies. Tracking trends and changes over time makes these assessments valuable.

### PNOĒ

The [PNOĒ](https://pnoe.com/) test measures breathing data, using a reliable method known as [ergospirometry](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9068094/).

It's the gold standard for professional athletes.

Testing 23 biomarkers that assess vital areas, including:

- Metabolism
- Cellular function
- Heart & lung fitness
- Fat-burning efficiency
- Biological age.

<img src="/uploads/2024/01/Tarquin-Stapa-doing-a-PNOE-Test-1024x576.webp" alt="Tarquin Stapa doing a PNOĒ Test — wearing a mask during a rowing session." class="max-w-lg mx-auto block rounded-lg my-6" loading="lazy" />

One thing though, I am no professional athlete (as you will see by some of my scores).

Great data on where my fitness levels were at before I started this indoor rowing challenge.

#### Windows of Trainability

A visual representation of my body's ability to train across four main areas: Endurance, Speed & Power, Strength, and Coordination & Skill.

When those four principals are green, it states my body is ready and able to do hard training. Since three are yellow and one red, I should consider modifying training intensities.

I can still train if it's red — it would just come at a greater physiological cost.

#### Overall Readiness

My readiness score before the rowing challenge was at 4.0, which is fair.

The Functional State data reveals my central nervous system (CNS) was fatigued at level 1.0. The CNS manages my body's activities. Intense training and heavy lifting strain it.

My cardiac system (CS) was ready to go at 7.0.

The Omegawave data recommended my heart rate training only go up to Aerobic Development (Z4).

Rowing is an aerobic workout so great to hear.

<img src="/uploads/2024/01/Omegawave-Initial-Assessment.-Cardiac-System.-Rowing-Machine-Challenge-1024x768.webp" alt="Omegawave — Initial Assessment. Cardiac System. Rowing Machine Challenge" class="max-w-lg mx-auto block rounded-lg my-6" loading="lazy" />

#### Autonomic Balance

The sympathetic nervous system triggers the fight or flight response in danger. While the parasympathetic nervous system calms the body afterwards. Both are in the green range for me.

The scattergram and histogram graphs are visual representations of heart rate variability (HRV). The rhythmogram depicts these points in the image.

[Garmin's Aerobic Training Effect (TE)](https://www.garmin.com/en-US/garmin-technology/running-science/physiological-measurements/aerobic-training-effect/) refers to the excess post-exercise oxygen consumption (EPOC) during a workout. Measured on a scale from 0 to 5, it considers your fitness level and training routines.

Aerobic exercise is continuous activity using oxygen. While anaerobic exercise is short, intense activity using muscle energy.
