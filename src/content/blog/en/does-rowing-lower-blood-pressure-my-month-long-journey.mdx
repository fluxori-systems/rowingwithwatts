Rowing is like a natural remedy; each stroke helps lower blood pressure and boosts your heart health.

 

I rowed 10,000m (32,808 ft.) every day for a month on a rowing machine. I wanted to understand the impact it would have on my health. So I tracked my daily biomarkers along the way. Blood pressure being one of those data points.

 

In this article I include my cardiac system assessments from before and after the rowing challenge. Look at timeline charts of my daily blood pressure measurements.

 

I also rate my aerobic training effect score for the month.

 

Finally, I compared my findings to research on how exercise activities affect blood pressure. The research reviewed aerobic exercise, High Impact Interval Training (HIIT) and isometric holds.

   Key Takeaways        

My systolic blood pressure decreased by 15%

 

My diastolic blood pressure decreased by 22%

 

Research shows all activities are beneficial for decreasing blood pressure, but isometric holds work best. 

   

### My Rowing Challenge

 

What happens when you [row 10k every day for a month](https://rowingwithwatts.com/rowing-10k-every-day-for-a-month/)? I decided to track the journey to find out. 

 

I did various before and after body and health assessments. Tracked my diet and daily body composition statistics.

 

I really wanted to track my blood pressure, especially after my mother recently had a heart attack. Thankfully, she’s recovering well.

 

This incident prompted me to explore how rowing might affect my blood pressure.

## Blood Pressure and Various Exercises

 

So where then does indoor rowing fall as an exercise at improving blood pressure? A 2023 study looked into this [[1](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37491419/)].

 

They broke exercises down into the below categories:

 
 
- Aerobic: Walking, Running, Cycling 
- HIIT: Sprint Interval, Aerobic Interval 
- Isometric: Handgrip, Leg Extension, Wall Squats 
 

Spoiler. They all work at reducing blood pressure. But some are better than others.

 

Here are the results on systolic and diastolic blood pressure.

#### **HIIT Exercise Benefits**

 

Based on the study data, HIIT exercises like sprint interval training show reductions in SBP by 5 mmHg and DBP by 3 mmHg.

 

HIIT rowing sessions, in my view, provides an efficient method for blood pressure control. Especially within shorter workout durations.
