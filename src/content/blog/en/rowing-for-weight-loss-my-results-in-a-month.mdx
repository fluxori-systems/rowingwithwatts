#### Initial InBody Assessment

 

The [InBody](https://inbody.com/en) test results provide a comprehensive analysis of your body composition, detailing the distribution of body fat, muscle mass, and water weight across different regions.

 

When you stand on the machine and hold the handles, it sends a small electric current through you. This current is used to measure body composition through a method called Bioelectrical Impedance Analysis.

 

<img src="/uploads/2024/01/Inbody-Machine-Model-270-768x1024.webp" alt="Inbody Machine - Model 270. A digital scale placed on a wooden floor." class="w-full mx-auto block rounded-lg my-6" loading="lazy" />

_The InBody Machine I Used - Model 270._

> Modern assessment machines provide good results but aren't in a completely controlled environment, so there may be discrepancies. Tracking trends and changes over time makes these assessments valuable.

You get quite a few data points from the assessment but I am going to focus on the segmental fat analysis.

Segmental analysis breaks down body composition into five key areas for a more detailed understanding. It measures fat percentage in each area: the torso (or trunk), left and right arm, left and right leg.

Here are my results:

<img src="/uploads/2024/01/Tarquin-Stapa-Initial-Inbody-Assessment-Segmetal-Fat-Analysis-lbs.-Rowing-Machine-Challenge-1-1024x958.webp" alt="Initial InBody Assessment - Segmental Fat Analysis" class="w-full mx-auto block rounded-lg my-6" loading="lazy" />

 

Looking at the infographic's data points, my arms and legs fat percentage was average and balanced. Great to see. The problem area was my trunk. A nice way of saying my belly fat was above the norm. Guess those chocolate chip cookie helpings had finally caught up.

## Does Rowing Intensity Affect Fat Loss?

 

The weight loss results were definitely in my favor for this rowing challenge. I wondered how much of it was down to being consistent or through the intensity from the activity.

 

A 2017 meta-analysis study looked to understand how Interval training and moderate-intensity aerobic workouts impact the body differently. This study was a deep dive and excluded any papers with a nutrition or supplementation component. 402 articles, 28 studies providing 35 comparisons were included [[3](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28513103/)].

 

This study reviewed controlled trials comparing high-intensity interval training (HIIT) or sprint interval training (SIT) with moderate-intensity continuous training (MICT) in terms of their impact on body fat percentage and total body fat mass over at least four weeks of intervention.

 

<img src="/uploads/2024/07/Adapted-from-Does-exercise-type-affect-fat-loss-n.d.-Examine.-httpsexamine.comcategoriesfat-lossfaqdoes-exercise-type-affect-fat-loss-1024x370.webp" alt="Infographic: Does exercise type affect fat loss - comparing moderate, high and sprint intensity" class="w-full mx-auto block rounded-lg my-6 w-full" loading="lazy" />

_Infographic - Does exercise type affect fat loss_

 

The study suggests that when targeting belly fat, the intensity of indoor rowing doesn't play a major role. Both high-intensity intervals and consistent, steady rowing decrease fat.

 

The key factor is the calories burned during the workout. Specifically, steady rowing sessions that burn more calories may be more effective at reducing body fat than high-intensity intervals. 

 

Basically, don't skip your training days. Showing up daily and getting on the rower matters. Whatever the intensity.
